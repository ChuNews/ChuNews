The Edinburgh People I Met Cannot Be Nicer - Part 2 of 3

Mark Chu July 12, 2026

I reserved the train tickets (LNER) for Edinburgh months beforehand.  If you want to use the train in the UK, make sure you do that.  First, buying the tickets right before costs significantly more; second, flying (especially the budget airlines) isn't as good, at least according to my son. 

Getting to the station was a bit tricky; we were lost for a bit.  But things all worked out.

I have to tell you about a toilet I went to on the way to the station though: As a few of the toilets I'd been to, it required a fee, and so I had to pay it with my phone to go through the gate.

I got in, used the toilet, then came back to the gate and was ready to get out.  But nope, the gate thought I should stay in there for a while.  No matter what I did and tried, neither of the two gates would open.  Did I miss something?  Should I have done something?  Or is this a "Five bucks to get in, ten bucks to get out" situation?  I don't remember what I did, but I finally got out probably because I found the way, pure luck, or the gates finally decided they had their way with me.

I was out, but I had to wait for my wife.  Then one by one, other people came to the trap too.  I tried my best to help them, and now I seriously forget what I did, but I did help a few.  They were nice enough to give me a friendly look (and a nasty look at the naughty gates).

We reached the station.  It looked nice, but not a lot of seats were available.  Luckily, we found a few.  The toilets were totally packed though; there was a queue even for men!

We got lunch at the station.  Well, not a lot of options, and not the best.  Here's mine, you be the judge.

We got on the train, but got confused by the seating.  People were kind enough to help us get to the right coach.  Off we went!
