Touch of Life

Mark Chu July 20, 2026

Chapter 1

Orion woke and immediately touched his forehead. Huh, I’m alive... wait, I’m standing? Looking around, he realized he was in the middle of a narrow street. It was a dark night, with a dim, yellowish streetlight in the distance casting blurry shadows on the trees lining the road. The air felt somehow cold, as it had just rained. The road was still wet, and water was dripping from the leaves.

How did I get here? It took him a while to concentrate. Wait, I remember someone pointing a gun at me; then something hit my forehead like I’d been kicked by a horse… He touched his forehead again: it was smooth and dry, with no hole or blood. Who was it? It had felt like a bullet drilled into my head… He felt his arms, hands, legs, and toes. Everything was fine.  

This unpaved street was in the middle of nowhere; no houses or buildings were in sight, not even a stop sign or speed limit. Not knowing what else to do, he started walking. The sound of his footsteps on the dirt and small rocks scratched into the eerie silence.

A car approached from behind, but he didn’t look. When the car slowly passed him, his peripheral vision caught a glimpse of a young kid in the passenger seat looking at him funny.

He walked for about half an hour before seeing some dingy wooden houses on the left. They were old and run-down, with broken doorsteps and cracked windows. A bit further, some people were lounging, smoking, or talking around a fire. They were all in thick coats. They became quiet when he walked near them, staring at him as though seeing a ghost.

He didn’t know why, but he felt compelled to keep walking.

There were more run-down houses and ragged people. A group of three glared in his direction, then the one with a beard coughed. They walked up to block his way. “Hey bro, what’s up?” the bearded one said.

“Not much,” Orion replied. A hint of foul smell mixing tobacco, cheap booze, and other nameless sour odors like sweat dried in the sun found its way to his nose.

“You ain’t cold? What are you doing out so late around here?”

“I don’t know.”

They looked at each other and smirked. “Alright, you got some cash? My bro here needs a few dimes for his child support, you know what it’s like.”

Orion knew this was a robbery, but a strange calmness lodged in his chest, or maybe a lack of emotions. He searched his pocket, fished out his wallet, and opened it. “Sorry, no cash.”

“Plastic will do, too,” the bearded man laughed.

“But that’s mine.”

“You sure about that?” Another guy with a tattoo of a dragon on his right forearm pulled out a big knife.

Orion shrugged.

“Oh,” they acted surprised, looked at each other and laughed. The third guy, who had two nose rings, said, “So you wanna play it the hard way, huh? Tough guy?”

People started gathering. “Give them what they want, it’s not worth it,” some guy shouted.

“Listen to them, bro,” the bearded man said.

Orion put his wallet back into his pocket.

The tattooed guy pointed that big knife right at Orion’s face, only two inches away from his nose. “Give.”

Why isn’t my heart pounding? Shouldn’t I be scared? Orion replied, “We don’t have to do this… how about I buy you drinks? Dinner?”   

“Shut the fuck up and give me the fucking wallet,” the tattooed guy said in a low, threatening voice. “Another word and you’ll be crying for your mommy.”

“Listen…”

The knife slashed through Orion’s right arm, from bicep to wrist. Some among the crowd screamed. Then absolute silence.

“How…” The tattooed guy’s eyes grew big. Orion looked at his right arm, then looked up at the three guys; his face turned a little paler with more puzzlement.

“You didn’t cut him?” the nose ring guy asked.

“I did, I did, I…” The tattooed guy was shaking a little, then yelled, “Fuck!” and slashed across Orion’s right arm and his abdomen. It should have cut his stomach open.

The shirt tore, but his belly was untouched.

“Fuck! What’s this shit? Fuck you!” the tattooed guy shrieked at the top of his lungs, “I’m gonna kill you! You creep! Die now! Die!” he stabbed Orion in the chest five times, ten times, fifteen times… After he had stopped stabbing, Orion just stood there looking at him. The tattooed guy was panting and trembling.

“This is fucking creepy…” a voice came from the crowd. “Fuck this shit, I’m out of here!” Suddenly everyone started screaming and scattered. Orion was alone with his long ghostly shadow.

The next morning at around seven.

The sun's rays began to break through the clouds, painting the sky orange and yellow. A small restaurant, Green Creek, had a few guests enjoying their scrambled eggs and coffee. A young waitress was taking orders. The smell of freshly fried bacon and waffles wafted nearly two blocks away, drawing a few stray dogs and cats to linger nearby.

A bald middle-aged man with a mustache was sipping his coffee when he saw a miserable-looking man open the door. It was Orion. This was mid-December, but he only wore a torn navy blue short-sleeve shirt that barely held together, and a pair of jeans. Some frost formed on the tips of his hair. He didn’t seem cold though. The bald man stopped sipping.

Orion found a seat near him and sat down. Everyone in the restaurant couldn’t help but stare. The waitress very slowly walked up to Orion’s table.

“Can I have a cup of coffee and a shepherd’s pie?” Orion said.

“Sure… you okay, sir? Do you need anything? A blanket maybe?” the waitress asked.

“No, I’m fine; thank you for asking,” he grinned. Maybe the grin was to be reassuring, but everyone felt their hair standing up.

She poured a cup of coffee for him and slowly walked away, still looking at him.

A few more guests came in. The noise of people chatting, the busboy collecting plates and utensils, and the occasional clanking from the kitchen grew louder and more expansive, yet Orion’s attention stayed on his exposed torso, eyes drifting downward as he continued to feel it.

“Hey, sir,” the bald man said. “Wanna sit with me?”

Orion kept on touching his own chest, seeming distracted and disturbed.

“Sir?”

Orion raised his head, “Yes, sorry?”

“You want to sit here?” the bald man pointed at the seat across from him.
